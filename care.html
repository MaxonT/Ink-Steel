Pen Preservation

The art of maintaining writing instruments

Cleaning Rituals

Regular cleaning ensures smooth ink flow and prevents clogging. For most pens, a monthly cleaning is sufficient, but some inks (especially pigmented or iron gall) require more frequent attention.

Use only distilled water for cleaning - tap water can leave mineral deposits. For stubborn ink, a solution of 1 part household ammonia to 10 parts water can be used, followed by thorough rinsing.

Storage

Store pens horizontally or nib-up. Avoid extreme temperatures and direct sunlight which can damage materials. For long-term storage, clean thoroughly and consider silicone grease on threads.

Nib Care

Never apply excessive pressure - fountain pens should write under their own weight. For scratchy nibs, try writing on a brown paper bag to smooth the tipping material gently.

Material-Specific

Celluloid requires special care - keep away from other plastics and clean gently. Ebonite benefits from occasional polishing with a soft cloth to prevent oxidation.
